# SELF™ VOICE CONTRACTS

**Binding Agreements for Voice Data Handling**
*(Legal Document – Version 1.0)*

---

## Preamble

This Voice Contracts document establishes binding obligations for the handling, processing, and storage of voice data within the SELF system.

Voice data represents highly sensitive personal information requiring strict contractual protections to maintain user privacy and safety.

These contracts ensure that voice functionality serves therapeutic purposes without compromising individual autonomy or data security.

---

## Parties to the Contracts

**1. SELF System Operators**
- Responsible for voice data infrastructure
- Accountable for privacy compliance
- Obligated to implement voice data protections

**2. Voice Data Processors**
- Responsible for audio processing algorithms
- Accountable for data minimization
- Obligated to follow voice-specific safety protocols

**3. End Users**
- Responsible for consent and usage boundaries
- Accountable for appropriate voice submission
- Obligated to understand voice data implications

---

## Core Obligations

### Article 1: Voice Data Minimization

**1.1 No Raw Persistence**
Raw voice recordings shall not be persisted beyond operational necessity for processing.

**1.2 Processing-Only Storage**
Voice data storage is limited to the minimum duration required for therapeutic analysis and response generation.

**1.3 Automatic Deletion**
All voice data must be automatically deleted after processing completion, with no manual retention options.

### Article 2: Consent and Transparency

**2.1 Explicit Consent Required**
Voice recording requires explicit, informed user consent with clear explanation of data handling.

**2.2 Purpose Limitation**
Voice data may only be used for emotional support and therapeutic analysis, not for any other purposes.

**2.3 Transparency Obligations**
Users must be informed about voice data processing, storage duration, and deletion policies.

### Article 3: Security Requirements

**3.1 Encryption at Rest and Transit**
All voice data must be encrypted using industry-standard encryption both in storage and during transmission.

**3.2 Access Controls**
Voice data access is restricted to authorized processing systems only, with no human access permitted.

**3.3 Audit Logging**
All voice data operations must be logged for audit purposes, without storing the actual audio content.

### Article 4: Privacy Protections

**4.1 Anonymization Where Possible**
Voice data processing should anonymize identifiable characteristics when therapeutically appropriate.

**4.2 No Third-Party Sharing**
Voice data shall not be shared with third parties under any circumstances.

**4.3 Data Subject Rights**
Users retain full rights to access, correct, or delete their voice data processing history.

### Article 5: Safety Integration

**5.1 Doctrine Compliance**
Voice contracts must align with SELF doctrine, particularly regarding data persistence limitations.

**5.2 Crisis Protocol**
Voice data handling must support crisis intervention without compromising safety protocols.

**5.3 Containment Priority**
Voice functionality must prioritize user containment and safety over convenience or features.

---

## Implementation Requirements

### Article 6: Technical Standards

**6.1 Audio Processing Limits**
Voice processing algorithms must operate within defined computational boundaries to prevent excessive data retention.

**6.2 Fallback Mechanisms**
In case of processing failures, voice data must be immediately deleted without retry attempts.

**6.3 Monitoring and Alerts**
Systems must monitor voice data handling for compliance violations and alert operators immediately.

### Article 7: Legal Compliance

**7.1 Applicable Laws**
Voice contracts must comply with all relevant privacy and data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).

**7.2 International Standards**
Voice data handling must meet international privacy standards for audio data.

**7.3 Regular Audits**
Voice data systems must undergo regular independent audits for compliance.

---

## Violation Consequences

### Article 8: Enforcement

**8.1 Minor Violations**
- Immediate cessation of voice data processing
- Mandatory system audit and correction
- Public disclosure of violation

**8.2 Serious Violations**
- Complete shutdown of voice functionality
- License suspension
- Legal action for privacy breaches

**8.3 Systemic Failures**
- Permanent removal of voice features
- Loss of SELF certification
- Full system review and re-certification requirement

---

## Amendments and Updates

### Article 9: Contract Evolution

**9.1 Regular Review**
Voice contracts must be reviewed annually for adequacy against evolving privacy standards.

**9.2 Technology Updates**
Contracts must be updated to address new voice processing technologies and privacy threats.

**9.3 User Input**
User feedback on voice privacy concerns must be incorporated into contract updates.
