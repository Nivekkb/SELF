# SELF Documentation Organization Summary

**Date**: December 22, 2025  
**Purpose**: Document the public/private folder structure and access levels

## Folder Structure Overview

The SELF documentation has been organized into two main categories:

### 📁 PUBLIC FOLDER (`SELF_DOCS/public/`)
**Purpose**: Documents intended for public access, transparency, and general understanding

**Access Level**: Open to all stakeholders, public disclosure, transparency reporting

**Contents**:
- **Core Documentation**: About the Creator, Governance Overview, Public Summary
- **Safety Documentation**: Security Documentation, Safety Framework Alignment
- **Transparency Reports**: Public Transparency Report Template, Regulator Briefing
- **Business Documentation**: All business-facing documents (pricing, key generation, integration)
- **Executive Documentation**: Executive briefings, product overviews, strategic frameworks
- **Research Documentation**: Researcher guides, academic materials, framework analysis
- **Reviewer Documentation**: Public review procedures, safety validation frameworks
- **Developer Documentation**: Public API documentation, integration guides, technical specifications
- **Buyer Documentation**: Public purchasing information, buyer protection, sales criteria
- **Technical Documentation**: Public technical specifications, architecture, security models

### 📁 PRIVATE FOLDER (`SELF_DOCS/private/`)
**Purpose**: Sensitive legal, licensing, and compliance documents requiring restricted access

**Access Level**: Restricted to authorized personnel, legal teams, compliance officers

**Contents**:
- **Legal Documentation**: All licensing agreements, terms of service, privacy policies
- **Commercial Licenses**: Commercial licensing terms and agreements
- **Legal Defense**: Legal position documents and defense strategies
- **Compliance Contracts**: Safety enforcement contracts and compliance agreements
- **Covenants**: Free Forever Covenant and other binding agreements

## Security and Access Guidelines

### Public Folder Security
- **Encryption**: Standard encryption for data at rest and in transit
- **Backup**: Regular backups with standard retention policies
- **Access Logging**: Basic access logging for transparency metrics
- **Version Control**: Full version history for transparency and audit trails

### Private Folder Security
- **Encryption**: Enhanced encryption with access controls
- **Access Control**: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
- **Audit Logging**: Comprehensive audit trails for all access and modifications
- **Backup**: Secure backups with extended retention and recovery procedures
- **Legal Hold**: Capability for legal holds and compliance preservation

## Document Classification Standards

### Public Documents (🟢)
**Criteria**: 
- No sensitive legal or financial information
- Promotes transparency and public understanding
- Safe for public disclosure without risk
- Encourages stakeholder engagement and trust

**Examples**:
- Safety frameworks and guarantees
- Technical architecture (non-sensitive)
- Business practices and pricing
- Research findings and academic papers
- Transparency reports and public disclosures

### Private Documents (🔴)
**Criteria**:
- Contains sensitive legal, financial, or compliance information
- Could pose legal or competitive risks if disclosed
- Contains proprietary licensing or contractual terms
- Requires legal review before any disclosure

**Examples**:
- Terms of Service and Privacy Policies
- Commercial licensing agreements
- Legal defense strategies
- Compliance contracts and enforcement agreements
- Sensitive contractual terms
