# SELF Buyer Alignment Assessment

## Values-First Qualification Test for Potential SELF Partners

**Version 2.0** | **Assessment Duration: 45-60 minutes**

---

## Assessment Overview

### Purpose
This comprehensive alignment assessment helps potential SELF buyers evaluate their organization's compatibility with SELF's safety-first principles. The assessment measures alignment across nine critical dimensions to determine partnership suitability and identify improvement areas.

### Target Audience
- **Enterprise Leaders**: CEOs, CTOs, CISOs evaluating SELF adoption
- **AI Safety Officers**: Teams responsible for AI safety implementation
- **Procurement Teams**: Organizations considering SELF integration
- **Compliance Officers**: Teams ensuring regulatory alignment

### Assessment Structure
- **45 Multiple-Choice Questions** across 9 evaluation dimensions
- **Scoring Methodology** with weighted criteria
- **Qualification Thresholds** for different partnership levels
- **Actionable Recommendations** for improvement areas

### Completion Guidelines
- **Time Allocation**: 45-60 minutes for thoughtful responses
- **Honesty Required**: Accurate self-assessment is essential for partnership success
- **Team Collaboration**: Involve cross-functional stakeholders for comprehensive evaluation
- **Follow-Up Actions**: Use results to guide improvement initiatives

## Assessment Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Safety Commitment (15% Weight)
**Measures organizational dedication to AI safety as a core business priority**

#### Questions 1-5: Leadership and Budget Allocation
1. **Safety Leadership**: How is AI safety represented in executive leadership?
   - A) No dedicated safety leadership
   - B) Safety officer reports to IT/compliance
   - C) C-level safety executive with board oversight
   - D) Safety is core to company mission with CEO accountability

2. **Budget Allocation**: What percentage of AI budget is dedicated to safety?
   - A) <1% - Safety is an afterthought
   - B) 1-5% - Basic compliance requirements
   - C) 5-15% - Substantial safety investment
   - D) >15% - Safety-first organization with significant resources

3. **Safety Metrics**: How are safety outcomes measured and reported?
   - A) No formal safety metrics
   - B) Basic incident tracking only
   - C) Comprehensive safety KPIs with executive reporting
   - D) Safety metrics drive business decisions and compensation

4. **Safety Culture**: How is safety culture embedded in organizational values?
   - A) Safety is a compliance checkbox
   - B) Safety training is mandatory but not emphasized
   - C) Safety culture is actively promoted and rewarded
   - D) Safety is a core cultural value that defines the organization

5. **Crisis Response**: How prepared is the organization for AI safety incidents?
   - A) No incident response plan
   - B) Basic incident response procedures exist
   - C) Comprehensive crisis management with regular drills
   - D) Proactive risk management with continuous improvement

### Dimension 2: Ethical AI Principles (12% Weight)
**Evaluates adherence to established ethical AI frameworks and principles**

#### Questions 6-8: Ethical Frameworks and Bias Mitigation
6. **Ethical Framework Adoption**: Which ethical AI frameworks does your organization follow?
   - A) No formal ethical frameworks
   - B) Basic industry guidelines (some adoption)
   - C) Comprehensive framework (IEEE/NIST fully implemented)
   - D) Leadership in ethical AI with published frameworks

7. **Bias Mitigation**: How does your organization address algorithmic bias?
   - A) No systematic bias mitigation
   - B) Reactive bias correction when identified
   - C) Proactive bias detection and continuous monitoring
   - D) Bias mitigation is core to AI development lifecycle

8. **Transparency Practices**: How transparent is your organization about AI decision-making?
   - A) Limited transparency, decisions treated as proprietary
   - B) Basic explainability for regulatory compliance
   - C) Comprehensive transparency with public AI ethics reports
   - D) Open-source AI models with full algorithmic transparency

### Dimension 3: User Protection Focus (12% Weight)
**Assesses prioritization of user safety and harm prevention**

#### Questions 9-11: User-Centric Safety Approach
9. **Safety Design Priority**: How is user safety integrated into AI design?
   - A) Safety is considered after functionality is implemented
   - B) Safety requirements are documented but not prioritized
   - C) Safety is a primary design constraint from project inception
   - D) User safety drives all AI design and deployment decisions

10. **Harm Prevention Systems**: What systems exist to prevent AI-generated harm?
    - A) Basic content filtering only
    - B) Reactive harm mitigation when incidents occur
    - C) Proactive harm prevention with real-time monitoring
    - D) Comprehensive safety architecture preventing harm at source

11. **User Support Infrastructure**: How does your organization support users affected by AI issues?
    - A) Limited support through standard channels
    - B) Dedicated AI safety support team
    - C) 24/7 crisis support with mental health professionals
    - D) Proactive user safety monitoring and intervention

### Dimension 4: Technical Safety Capability (10% Weight)
**Evaluates technical competence in implementing AI safety systems**

#### Questions 12-14: Technical Infrastructure and Expertise
12. **Safety Engineering Team**: What is the size and expertise of your AI safety team?
    - A) No dedicated safety engineering resources
    - B) 1-2 people handling safety as secondary responsibility
    - C) Dedicated safety team with specialized expertise
    - D) Large safety engineering organization with research capabilities

13. **Monitoring Infrastructure**: How comprehensive is your AI monitoring capability?
    - A) Basic logging and alerting
    - B) Real-time performance monitoring
    - C) Comprehensive behavioral monitoring with anomaly detection
    - D) Advanced AI safety monitoring with predictive analytics

14. **Incident Response Technology**: What technology supports safety incident response?
    - A) Manual incident tracking
    - B) Basic incident management system
    - C) Automated incident detection and response orchestration
    - D) AI-powered incident prediction and automated remediation

### Dimension 5: SELF Integration Competence (8% Weight)

#### Questions 15-16: Integration Readiness
15. **Technical Integration Capability**: How experienced is your team with safety-critical system integration?
    - A) Limited integration experience
    - B) Basic API integration capabilities

16. **Safety System Architecture**: How well does your AI architecture support safety system integration?

### Dimension 6: Organizational Safety Culture (8% Weight)
**Measures how safety values are embedded in organizational culture**

    - A) No public commitment to ethical AI

    - D) Financial resources specifically allocated for long-term safety mission

24. **Technical Infrastructure**: How robust is your technical infrastructure for safety systems?
    - A) Basic infrastructure with safety limitations
    - B) Adequate infrastructure with safety considerations
    - C) Advanced infrastructure designed for safety-critical operations
    - D) State-of-the-art infrastructure optimized for AI safety systems

25. **Talent Pipeline**: How strong is your organization's AI safety talent pipeline?
    - A) Limited access to safety expertise
    - B) Basic safety training and recruitment
    - C) Strong safety talent development programs
    - D) Industry-leading safety research and development capabilities

## Scoring Methodology

### Dimension Weighting
- **Safety Commitment**: 15% (Leadership priority for safety)
- **Ethical AI Principles**: 12% (Foundation of responsible AI)
- **User Protection Focus**: 12% (Core user safety priority)
- **Technical Safety Capability**: 10% (Implementation competence)
- **SELF Integration Competence**: 8% (Specific integration readiness)
- **Organizational Safety Culture**: 8% (Cultural foundation)
- **Regulatory Compliance**: 7% (Legal and regulatory alignment)
- **Mission Compatibility**: 7% (Strategic alignment)
- **Resource Commitment**: 6% (Long-term sustainability)

### Scoring Scale
- **A Responses**: 1 point (Beginning/Non-compliant)
- **B Responses**: 2 points (Developing/Basic compliance)
- **C Responses**: 3 points (Proficient/Good compliance)
- **D Responses**: 4 points (Advanced/Excellent compliance)

### Qualification Thresholds

#### Full Approval (85-100%): Ready for Immediate Partnership
- **Score Range**: 340-400 points
- **Profile**: Organization demonstrates comprehensive alignment with SELF principles
- **Next Steps**: Proceed to contract negotiation and implementation planning

#### Conditional Approval (70-84%): Partnership with Development Requirements
- **Score Range**: 280-339 points
- **Profile**: Strong foundation with specific areas needing improvement
- **Next Steps**: Address identified gaps before full partnership

#### Development Partnership (55-69%): Significant Improvement Required
- **Score Range**: 220-279 points
- **Profile**: Moderate alignment with substantial development needs
- **Next Steps**: Implement comprehensive improvement plan with SELF guidance

#### Not Currently Aligned (0-54%): Substantial Transformation Required
- **Score Range**: 0-219 points
- **Profile**: Fundamental misalignment requiring organizational transformation
- **Next Steps**: Focus on basic AI safety foundation before considering SELF partnership

## Results Interpretation and Action Planning

### Score Analysis
1. **Calculate Total Score**: Sum all question points
2. **Review Dimension Scores**: Identify strengths and improvement areas
3. **Compare to Thresholds**: Determine qualification level
4. **Prioritize Improvements**: Focus on lowest-scoring dimensions

### Dimension-by-Dimension Analysis
Review your scores in each dimension to understand specific improvement needs:

- **Low Safety Commitment**: Focus on leadership development and budget allocation
- **Weak Ethical Frameworks**: Implement comprehensive ethical AI policies
- **Poor User Protection**: Develop user-centric safety programs
- **Limited Technical Capability**: Build safety engineering expertise
- **Integration Readiness Issues**: Develop safety system integration skills
- **Cultural Challenges**: Foster safety-first organizational culture
- **Compliance Gaps**: Strengthen regulatory compliance programs
- **Mission Misalignment**: Align business objectives with safety goals
- **Resource Constraints**: Secure long-term safety investment commitment

### Action Planning Framework

#### Immediate Actions (0-30 Days)
- **Results Communication**: Share assessment results with leadership
- **Quick Wins Identification**: Find high-impact, low-effort improvements
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Build internal support for safety initiatives
- **Resource Assessment**: Evaluate available improvement resources

#### Short-Term Improvements (1-6 Months)
- **Priority Gap Closure**: Address highest-impact improvement areas
- **Training Programs**: Implement safety education and skill development
- **Process Development**: Create safety-focused organizational processes
- **Pilot Programs**: Test safety improvements in limited scope

#### Long-Term Transformation (6-18 Months)
- **Cultural Change**: Embed safety values throughout organization
- **Capability Building**: Develop comprehensive safety expertise
- **Strategic Alignment**: Integrate safety into core business strategy
- **Industry Leadership**: Position organization as AI safety leader

### Partnership Readiness Milestones

#### Bronze Level (Conditional Approval Achieved)
- Basic safety infrastructure implemented
- Key personnel trained in safety principles
- Initial safety policies and procedures documented
- Basic compliance with regulatory requirements

#### Silver Level (Full Approval Preparation)
- Comprehensive safety culture established
- Advanced safety capabilities developed
- Strong safety metrics and reporting implemented
- Proven track record of safety incident management

#### Gold Level (Strategic Partnership Ready)
- Industry-leading safety practices implemented
- Significant safety research and development contributions
- Comprehensive safety ecosystem established
- Strategic alignment between business and safety missions
