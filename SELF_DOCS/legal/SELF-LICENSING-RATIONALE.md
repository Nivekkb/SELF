# Why SELF is Licensed This Way: A Guide for Buyers

## The Core Principle

**SELF is not just software—it's a safety-critical framework for emotional state management. Our licensing model reflects this responsibility.**

---

## 1. Doctrine Binding: Why It's Non-Negotiable

### The Problem
Emotional safety systems face unique risks:
- **False calm detection**: Users may appear stable when they're not
- **Premature normalization**: Systems may release users too soon
- **Uncertainty masking**: Polite language can hide distress
- **Abuse potential**: Bad actors may try to manipulate safety protocols

### Our Solution
The SELF Design Doctrine is **binding**, not advisory, because:

✅ **Consistency prevents harm**: Uniform safety protocols across all implementations
✅ **No race to the bottom**: Prevents competitive pressure to relax safety standards
✅ **Legal defensibility**: Creates clear standards of care for liability protection
✅ **User trust**: Ensures all SELF implementations meet minimum safety requirements

**Without Doctrine binding, SELF becomes just another chatbot framework.**

---

## 2. Version Compatibility: Why You Can't Mix Versions

### The Rule
> "Licensee may remain on an accepted Doctrine version, but cannot claim compatibility with newer SELF releases."

### Why This Matters

**Safety is not backward compatible:**

| Scenario | Risk | Our Protection |
|----------|------|----------------|
| Old Doctrine + New SELF | Safety gaps from outdated protocols | Require current Doctrine for new releases |
| New Doctrine + Old SELF | Missing critical safety improvements | Prevent false compatibility claims |
| Mixed implementations | Inconsistent safety standards | Enforce uniform compliance |

**Example:** If v2.0 adds false calm detection but you're on v1.5 Doctrine, you cannot claim your implementation meets v2.0 safety standards.
