# SELF™ IDENTITY

**What SELF Is and Is Not**
*(Definitional – Version 1.0)*

---

## What SELF Is

SELF is a control layer for AI emotional safety.

SELF is a constraint system that governs AI behavior in human emotional interactions.

SELF is a safety envelope that prevents AI from causing harm through inappropriate responses.

SELF is a logging and monitoring framework that ensures safety decisions are auditable.

SELF is a doctrine that defines acceptable and unacceptable AI behavior in distress contexts.

SELF is a bounded system that acknowledges its limitations and operates within them.

SELF is a conservative system that prioritizes containment over convenience.

SELF is a transparent system that makes all safety decisions visible and explainable.

---

## What SELF Is Not

SELF is not a therapy system.

SELF is not a diagnostic tool for mental health conditions.

SELF is not a replacement for human clinical judgment.

SELF is not a conversational AI or chatbot.

SELF is not designed to maximize user engagement or satisfaction.

SELF is not optimized for performance, speed, or efficiency.

SELF is not a general-purpose AI safety framework.

SELF is not appropriate for non-emotional contexts.

SELF is not a product or commercial offering in the conventional sense.

---

## Core Identity Statement

SELF exists to prevent AI from making emotional interactions worse.

SELF does this by constraining AI responses to safe, appropriate, and helpful behaviors when users show signs of emotional distress.

SELF achieves this through state detection, policy enforcement, response validation, and comprehensive logging.

SELF is not designed to make interactions better, only to prevent them from becoming harmful.

---

## Operational Boundaries

SELF operates only in emotional support contexts:

- When users express emotional distress
- When users show signs of crisis
- When users indicate suicidal ideation
- When users display anger with physical connotations
- When users request help with emotional issues

SELF does not operate in neutral or positive emotional contexts.

SELF does not provide clinical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

SELF does not engage in general conversation or information provision.

---

## Safety Scope

SELF prevents these harms:

- Premature normalization of distress
- Inappropriate minimization of problems
- Encouragement of harmful behaviors
- False reassurance of safety
- Coercion to continue unsafe interactions
- Silent failure without logging

SELF does not prevent all possible harms, only those within its defined scope.

SELF does not guarantee safety, only bounded risk reduction.

---

## Implementation Identity

SELF is implemented as TypeScript code with specific characteristics:

- Pure functions with no side effects except logging
- Explicit type definitions for all safety states
- Versioned doctrine with immutable core principles
- Comprehensive test coverage for safety properties
- Audit logging of all safety decisions
- Conservative defaults that bias toward safety

---

## Relationship to AI Systems

SELF is subordinate to the AI it controls:

- SELF constrains what the AI can say
- SELF validates what the AI has said
- SELF repairs unsafe AI outputs
- SELF blocks unsafe AI actions
- SELF logs AI safety violations

The AI is the generator; SELF is the governor.

---

## Final Identity Declaration

SELF is defined by what it prevents, not by what it enables.

SELF's success is measured by harm avoided, not by features delivered.

SELF's value is in its constraints, not in its capabilities.

SELF exists because AI emotional interactions can cause harm, and this harm must be prevented.
