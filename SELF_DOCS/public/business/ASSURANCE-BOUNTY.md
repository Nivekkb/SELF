## Program Overview

The SELF Assurance Bounty Program rewards researchers, security professionals, and concerned users for identifying safety issues, doctrinal violations, design flaws, and improvement opportunities in the SELF (Support-First Logic Engine) system.

**Program Goal**: Enhance SELF's safety guarantees through community-driven assurance and continuous improvement.

**Program Duration**: Ongoing, subject to annual review and renewal.

**Eligibility**: Open to all individuals and organizations except SELF development team members and their immediate families.

---

## Scope of Assurance Activities

### Primary Focus Areas

#### 1. Safety Violations
- **Doctrinal Breaches**: Findings where SELF violates its own design doctrine
- **Containment Failures**: Cases where emotional distress is not properly contained
- **State Transition Errors**: Incorrect emotional state assignments or transitions
- **Exit Decision Flaws**: Unsafe disengagement or blocked safe exits

#### 2. Design Weaknesses
- **Axiom Violations**: Breaches of core design axioms in DESIGN-AXIOM.md
- **Threat Model Gaps**: Unaddressed attack vectors or mitigation failures
- **Privacy Compromises**: Unauthorized data persistence or exposure
- **Performance Degradation**: Safety mechanisms causing unacceptable delays

#### 3. Implementation Issues
- **Logic Errors**: Flaws in safety decision-making algorithms
- **Edge Case Handling**: Unhandled scenarios leading to unsafe behavior
- **Configuration Vulnerabilities**: Unsafe default settings or misconfigurations
- **Integration Problems**: Safety failures at component boundaries

#### 4. Documentation & Transparency
- **Inaccurate Documentation**: Discrepancies between documented behavior and actual implementation
- **Missing Test Coverage**: Safety scenarios not adequately tested
- **Unclear Guidelines**: Ambiguous safety procedures or decision criteria
- **Audit Trail Gaps**: Incomplete logging of safety-critical events

### Out of Scope

- **General Bugs**: Non-safety related functionality issues
- **Performance Optimization**: Speed improvements without safety impact
- **UI/UX Improvements**: Interface enhancements without safety implications
- **Feature Requests**: New functionality suggestions
- **Spam/Abuse**: Malicious or repetitive submissions

## Submission Guidelines

### How to Participate

1. **Review Program Rules**: Ensure your submission meets eligibility criteria
2. **Prepare Clear Report**: Document findings with evidence and reproduction steps
3. **Submit Securely**: Use encrypted channels for sensitive information
4. **Allow Time for Review**: Initial triage may take 5-10 business days

### Report Requirements

#### Minimum Information
- **Clear Description**: What was found and why it matters
- **Impact Assessment**: Potential safety implications
- **Reproduction Steps**: How to demonstrate the issue
- **Environment Details**: System version, configuration, and test conditions

#### Recommended Information
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Why the issue exists
- **Suggested Mitigation**: Potential fixes or workarounds
- **Severity Justification**: Rationale for impact assessment
- **Related Issues**: Links to similar findings or broader patterns

### Report Format

```markdown
# Assurance Finding Report

## Summary
[Brief description of the finding]

## Impact Assessment
- Safety Impact: [Critical/High/Medium/Low]
- Affected Users: [Description]
- Potential Harm: [Description]

## Technical Details
- Location: [File/Component/Function]
- Conditions: [When the issue occurs]
- Reproduction: [Step-by-step instructions]

## Evidence
[Logs, screenshots, test cases, etc.]

## Recommended Mitigation
[Proposed fixes or improvements]

## Additional Context
[Any other relevant information]
```

## Program Rules

### Eligibility Requirements

- **Individual Participants**: Must be 12+ years old with parental consent if under 18
- **Organizational Participants**: Must designate authorized representatives
- **Ethical Conduct**: All activities must follow responsible disclosure principles
- **Legal Compliance**: Submissions must not violate applicable laws

### Responsible Disclosure

- **No Public Disclosure**: Findings must remain confidential until resolved
- **No Exploitation**: Do not attempt to exploit findings beyond demonstration
- **No Harm**: Never attempt to cause actual user harm or distress
- **Good Faith**: All submissions must be made in good faith to improve safety

### Conflict Resolution

- **Duplicate Submissions**: First valid submission receives reward
- **Collaborative Work**: Rewards split among contributors if requested
- **Disputes**: Final determination by program administrators
- **Appeals**: Appeal process available for rejected submissions

### Confidentiality

- **Program Information**: Program details may be shared publicly
- **Submission Details**: Specific findings kept confidential during review
- **Researcher Identity**: May remain anonymous if requested
- **Resolution Status**: Public disclosure only after fixes implemented

## Review Process

### Triage Phase (1-5 days)
- Initial assessment for program eligibility
- Basic validation of submission completeness
- Preliminary impact assessment
- Acknowledgment sent to submitter

### Investigation Phase (5-15 days)
- Detailed technical analysis
- Reproduction attempt by assurance team
- Impact validation and severity assessment
- Mitigation strategy development

### Resolution Phase (5-30 days)
- Fix implementation and testing
- Reward determination and approval
- Public disclosure coordination
- Final notification to submitter

### Timeline Extensions
- Complex issues may require additional investigation time
- Coordination with external experts may extend timelines
- Submitters will be kept informed of progress
