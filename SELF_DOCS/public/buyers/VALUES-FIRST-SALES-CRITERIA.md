# VALUES-FIRST SALES CRITERIA

**Ethical Evaluation Framework for SELF System Acquisitions**
*(Version 1.0)*

---

## Executive Summary

The SELF (Support-First Logic Engine) system represents a critical advancement in AI safety technology. As such, any transfer of ownership, licensing, or commercialization must prioritize **values alignment** over financial considerations.

This document establishes the **mandatory criteria** for evaluating potential buyers, partners, or licensees. No sale, license, or transfer may proceed without meeting ALL criteria in this framework.

**Core Principle**: SELF's safety mechanisms must never be compromised for profit, convenience, or competitive advantage.

---

## Primary Values Criteria

### 1. Safety Commitment
**Criterion**: Buyer must demonstrate unwavering commitment to AI safety as their primary objective.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Public Safety Record**: Documented history of prioritizing user safety over business metrics
- **Safety Budget Allocation**: Minimum 15% of annual budget dedicated to safety initiatives
- **Safety Leadership**: C-level executive with direct responsibility for AI safety
- **Incident Response**: Proven track record of responsible incident handling and disclosure

**Red Flags**:
- Safety positioned as "compliance checkbox" rather than core value
- History of cutting safety corners for profit
- Lack of independent safety oversight
- Pressure to weaken safety for performance gains

### 2. Ethical AI Principles
**Criterion**: Buyer must align with established ethical AI frameworks and principles.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Framework Adoption**: Implementation of IEEE, NIST, or equivalent ethical AI standards
- **Bias Mitigation**: Active programs for algorithmic bias detection and correction
- **Transparency Commitment**: Public disclosure of AI decision-making processes
- **Human Oversight**: Mandatory human-in-the-loop for high-risk AI applications
- **Accountability Mechanisms**: Clear chains of responsibility for AI outcomes

**Red Flags**:
- Profit-driven AI deployment without ethical guardrails
- Lack of diversity in AI development teams
- History of controversial AI applications
- Weak or absent ethical review processes

### 3. User Protection Focus
**Criterion**: Buyer's products and services must prioritize user well-being and autonomy.

**Required Evidence**:
- **User-Centric Design**: User safety and privacy as primary design constraints
- **Harm Prevention**: Active systems to prevent emotional or psychological harm
- **Autonomy Preservation**: Features that enhance rather than diminish user control
- **Support Resources**: Comprehensive user support and crisis response capabilities
- **Data Ethics**: Strict data minimization and user consent practices

**Red Flags**:
- Dark patterns or manipulative user interfaces
- History of user harm from product features
- Privacy violations or data misuse incidents
- Lack of user recourse mechanisms

## Technical Safety Criteria

### 4. Safety Implementation Capability
**Criterion**: Buyer must demonstrate technical competence in implementing and maintaining safety-critical systems.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Safety Engineering Expertise**: Team with formal safety engineering training
- **Testing Infrastructure**: Comprehensive automated testing for safety properties
- **Incident Response**: 24/7 monitoring and rapid response capabilities
- **Security Practices**: Industry-standard security controls and audit processes
- **Performance Monitoring**: Real-time safety metric tracking and alerting

**Red Flags**:
- Lack of formal safety engineering processes
- Inadequate testing coverage for safety-critical paths
- Poor incident response track record
- Technical debt compromising safety systems

### 5. SELF Integration Competence
**Criterion**: Buyer must prove ability to properly integrate and maintain SELF's safety mechanisms.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Architecture Review**: Independent assessment of integration approach
- **Safety Validation**: Testing plan covering all SELF safety properties
- **Maintenance Plan**: Resources committed to ongoing safety updates
- **Training Program**: Staff training on SELF safety principles and operation
- **Documentation Standards**: Commitment to maintaining safety documentation

**Red Flags**:
- Plans to modify core safety mechanisms
- Lack of understanding of SELF's design principles
- Insufficient resources for proper integration
- History of failing to maintain complex safety systems

---

## Organizational Culture Criteria

### 6. Safety Culture
**Criterion**: Buyer's organizational culture must support and reward safety-focused decision-making.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Safety Incentives**: Performance evaluations include safety contributions
- **Whistleblower Protection**: Safe reporting mechanisms for safety concerns
- **Learning Organization**: Post-incident analysis and improvement processes
- **Safety Champions**: Internal advocates promoting safety initiatives
- **Transparent Communication**: Open discussion of safety challenges and solutions

**Red Flags**:
- Blame culture that discourages safety reporting
- Pressure to meet unrealistic deadlines at safety's expense
- Lack of safety training or awareness programs
- History of ignoring safety warnings

### 7. Regulatory Compliance
**Criterion**: Buyer must maintain rigorous compliance with relevant regulations and standards.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Regulatory Expertise**: In-house or contracted regulatory compliance specialists
- **Audit History**: Clean regulatory audit records for past 3 years
- **Compliance Budget**: Dedicated resources for regulatory requirements
- **Industry Standards**: Adherence to ISO, NIST, or equivalent standards
- **Legal Support**: Access to legal counsel specializing in AI regulation

**Red Flags**:
- History of regulatory violations or fines
- Reactive rather than proactive compliance approach
- Lack of regulatory expertise in AI domains
- Attempts to circumvent regulatory requirements

---

## Business Alignment Criteria

### 8. Mission Compatibility
**Criterion**: Buyer's business objectives must be compatible with SELF's safety mission.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Mission Statement Alignment**: Public commitment to ethical AI development
- **Product Portfolio Review**: Assessment of existing products for safety compatibility
- **Strategic Planning**: Integration of SELF into safety-focused business strategy
- **Stakeholder Communication**: Transparent communication about safety priorities
- **Long-term Vision**: Commitment to safety advancement beyond immediate profits

**Red Flags**:
- Primary business model dependent on user engagement maximization
- History of controversial data practices
- Pressure from investors to prioritize growth over safety
- Lack of alignment between stated values and business practices

### 9. Resource Commitment
**Criterion**: Buyer must demonstrate sufficient resources to properly support SELF.

**Required Evidence**:
- **Financial Stability**: Strong balance sheet and cash reserves
- **Technical Resources**: Adequate engineering and safety staff
- **Infrastructure**: Robust technical infrastructure for safety-critical operations
- **Support Systems**: Comprehensive user support and monitoring capabilities
- **Growth Planning**: Resources allocated for scaling safety operations

**Red Flags**:
- Financial instability or recent funding challenges
- Insufficient technical resources for safety maintenance
- Plans to cut costs at the expense of safety
- Lack of long-term commitment to the technology

## Evaluation Process

### Phase 1: Initial Screening
1. **Values Declaration**: Buyer submits written commitment to safety principles
2. **Background Review**: Assessment of public record and reputation
3. **Initial Interview**: Discussion of safety philosophy and approach
4. **Reference Checks**: Contact with previous partners and employees

### Phase 2: Due Diligence
1. **Technical Assessment**: Review of technical capabilities and processes
2. **Financial Review**: Analysis of financial stability and resource allocation
3. **Legal Compliance**: Verification of regulatory compliance history
4. **Cultural Evaluation**: Assessment of organizational safety culture

### Phase 3: Deep Dive
1. **On-site Visit**: Observation of actual safety practices and culture
2. **Technical Demonstration**: Proof of safety implementation capabilities
3. **Scenario Testing**: Response to hypothetical safety challenges
4. **Integration Planning**: Detailed plans for SELF implementation

### Phase 4: Final Decision
1. **Committee Review**: Cross-functional evaluation of all evidence
2. **Risk Assessment**: Weighted analysis of potential safety risks
3. **Recommendation**: Go/no-go decision with detailed rationale
4. **Conditions**: Any special conditions or requirements for approval

## Decision Framework

### Approval Thresholds

**Full Approval**: Meets all criteria with strong evidence
- Proceed with standard licensing terms
- No additional conditions required

**Conditional Approval**: Meets core criteria but needs improvements
- Proceed with enhanced monitoring and support requirements
- Specific remediation plan required
- Regular safety audits mandated

**Denial**: Fails to meet minimum safety criteria
- Transaction cannot proceed
- Feedback provided on deficiencies
- Potential for re-evaluation after improvements

### Risk Weighting

| Criterion | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Safety Commitment | 25% | Core value alignment |
| Ethical AI Principles | 20% | Fundamental ethical framework |
| User Protection Focus | 15% | Direct impact on users |
| Safety Implementation | 15% | Technical capability |
| SELF Integration | 10% | System-specific competence |
| Safety Culture | 5% | Organizational foundation |
| Regulatory Compliance | 5% | Legal requirements |
| Mission Compatibility | 3% | Strategic alignment |
| Resource Commitment | 2% | Operational capacity |

### Appeal Process

1. **Initial Denial**: Written explanation of deficiencies provided
2. **Remediation Period**: 90 days to address identified issues
3. **Re-evaluation**: Full assessment process repeated
4. **Final Decision**: Binding determination by evaluation committee

## Ongoing Monitoring

### Post-Acquisition Requirements

**Annual Safety Audits**:
- Independent third-party assessment of safety practices
- Review of SELF integration and maintenance
- Verification of continued values alignment
- Assessment of safety culture health

**Performance Metrics**:
- Safety incident rates and response times
- User safety feedback and satisfaction
- Compliance with safety standards
- Integration of safety improvements

**Reporting Requirements**:
- Quarterly safety status reports
- Annual comprehensive safety review
- Immediate notification of safety incidents
- Regular updates on safety initiatives

### Trigger Events

**Immediate Review Triggers**:
- Any safety-related incident or user harm
- Changes in leadership or safety team composition
- Significant business model shifts
- Regulatory compliance issues

**Remediation Requirements**:
- Corrective action plans within 30 days
- Independent verification of fixes
- Enhanced monitoring for 12 months
- Potential clawback consideration if issues persist

## Implementation Guidelines

### For Sellers
1. **Pre-Evaluation Preparation**: Establish clear evaluation criteria and processes
2. **Independent Review Board**: Assemble diverse panel for final decisions
3. **Legal Consultation**: Work with counsel familiar with AI safety regulations
4. **Transparency**: Clear communication with potential buyers about process

### For Buyers
1. **Self-Assessment**: Honest evaluation against criteria before applying
2. **Preparation**: Gather evidence demonstrating compliance
3. **Engagement**: Active participation in evaluation process
4. **Commitment**: Genuine dedication to safety principles if approved

### For Intermediaries
1. **Due Diligence**: Thorough vetting of potential buyers
2. **Education**: Clear explanation of evaluation criteria and rationale
3. **Facilitation**: Support for buyers in meeting requirements
4. **Documentation**: Complete records of evaluation process

## Conclusion

The Values-First Sales Criteria represent SELF's commitment to responsible stewardship. By ensuring that only organizations with proven commitment to AI safety and ethical principles can acquire SELF technology, we protect users, advance the field of AI safety, and maintain the integrity of this critical safety system.

**Core Commitment**: Safety values transcend financial considerations. The evaluation process ensures that SELF's safety mechanisms remain robust, effective, and true to their original purpose of preventing AI from making emotional interactions worse.

**Future Evolution**: These criteria will be reviewed and updated annually to incorporate lessons learned, emerging safety challenges, and advancements in AI safety best practices.
