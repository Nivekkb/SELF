# SELF Regulatory & Standards Positioning

## Executive Summary

This document provides an honest assessment of SELF's regulatory positioning, including what regulations it aligns with, which it 
intentionally avoids triggering, and when regulatory engagement would be appropriate. SELF is designed to operate within existing 
regulatory frameworks while providing safety enhancements that exceed current requirements.

## What SELF Is

### 1. **Constitutional AI Safety Framework**
- **Definition**: A self-governing framework that enforces immutable safety principles
- **Purpose**: Ensures AI systems maintain safety boundaries through constitutional constraints
- **Scope**: Emotional AI applications and mental health support systems

### 2. **Safety Control Layer**
- **Definition**: A middleware safety system that sits between AI models and user interfaces
- **Purpose**: Enforces safety protocols without modifying underlying AI models
- **Scope**: Any AI system providing emotional support or mental health assistance

### 3. **Compliance Enhancement System**
- **Definition**: Framework that enhances existing regulatory compliance
- **Purpose**: Provides additional safety measures beyond minimum legal requirements
- **Scope**: Organizations seeking to exceed regulatory standards

### 4. **Risk Mitigation Framework**
- **Definition**: Systematic approach to identifying and mitigating AI safety risks
- **Purpose**: Proactive risk management for emotional AI applications
- **Scope**: High-risk AI applications in healthcare and mental health contexts

## What SELF Is Not

### 1. **Not a Medical Device**
- **Why**: SELF does not diagnose, treat, or prevent medical conditions
- **Regulatory Avoidance**: Intentionally avoids FDA medical device classification
- **Positioning**: Safety enhancement layer, not therapeutic intervention
- **Evidence**: No medical claims, no treatment protocols, no diagnostic capabilities

### 2. **Not a Clinical Decision Support System**
- **Why**: SELF does not provide clinical recommendations or treatment decisions
- **Regulatory Avoidance**: Avoids FDA's Clinical Decision Support (CDS) regulations
- **Positioning**: Safety monitoring and boundary enforcement only
- **Evidence**: No clinical decision-making, no treatment suggestions

### 3. **Not a Mental Health Treatment System**
- **Why**: SELF focuses on safety and support, not therapy or treatment
- **Regulatory Avoidance**: Avoids mental health treatment licensing requirements
- **Positioning**: Emotional support with safety boundaries
- **Evidence**: No therapeutic interventions, no treatment plans, no clinical protocols

### 4. **Not a Diagnostic Tool**
- **Why**: SELF does not identify or classify medical or psychological conditions
- **Regulatory Avoidance**: Avoids diagnostic tool regulations and licensing
- **Positioning**: State detection for safety purposes only
- **Evidence**: No diagnostic criteria, no condition identification, no medical classification
