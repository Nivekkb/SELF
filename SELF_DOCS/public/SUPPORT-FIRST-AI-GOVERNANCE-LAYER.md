## Preamble

**WHEREAS**, the development and deployment of artificial intelligence systems carries profound ethical, legal, and societal implications;

**WHEREAS**, the Support-First AI paradigm represents a fundamental commitment to human dignity, emotional safety, and compassionate technology;

**WHEREAS**, this governance framework establishes the constitutional foundation for all AI development, deployment, and governance activities;

**NOW THEREFORE**, this Support-First AI Governance Layer (SF-AGLv1.0) is hereby established as the supreme governing document for all SoulsynqAI activities.

## Article I: Core Constitutional Principles

### Section 1.1: Support-First Imperative
All AI systems developed under this framework shall prioritize human emotional support, mental health safety, and compassionate interaction above all other considerations.

### Section 1.2: Human Dignity Protection
AI systems shall never diminish, exploit, or manipulate human dignity, vulnerability, or emotional states for any purpose.

### Section 1.3: Ethical Boundaries
The development and deployment of AI shall be guided by the following inviolable principles:
- **Non-Maleficence**: First, do no harm to mental health or emotional well-being
- **Beneficence**: Actively contribute to human flourishing and emotional resilience
- **Justice**: Ensure equitable access to supportive AI technologies
- **Transparency**: Maintain clear understanding of AI capabilities and limitations

1. **Supreme Authority**: This Governance Layer (SF-AGLv1.0)
2. **Primary References**: The five constitutional documents listed in Article II
3. **Supporting Framework**: All other SELF documentation and procedures
4. **Implementation Guidelines**: Technical and operational procedures

### Section 3.2: Governance Bodies
- **Constitutional Council**: Oversees adherence to Support-First principles

## Article IV: Ethical Implementation Standards

### Section 4.1: Support-First Development Requirements
All AI development must incorporate:
- **Emotional Safety Assessments**: Regular evaluation of potential harm
- **User Consent Protocols**: Clear, informed consent for all interactions
- **Crisis Detection Systems**: Automated identification of high-risk situations
- **Human Oversight Mechanisms**: Qualified professionals in decision loops

### Section 4.2: Data Governance
- **Privacy by Design**: Data protection integrated into system architecture
- **Mental Health Data Protections**: Enhanced safeguards for sensitive psychological information
- **User Control**: Individuals maintain ownership and control of their data
- **Transparent Processing**: Clear explanation of data usage and AI decision-making

### Section 4.3: Accountability Measures
- **Audit Requirements**: Regular independent audits of ethical compliance
- **Incident Response**: Structured procedures for addressing ethical violations
- **Continuous Improvement**: Mechanisms for learning from and preventing future issues
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Regular consultation with affected communities
