# SELF™ NO-DRIFT CONTRACT

**Binding Agreement for Principle Preservation**
*(Legal Document – Version 1.0)*

---

## Preamble

This No-Drift Contract ("Contract") establishes binding obligations to prevent drift from SELF's foundational principles.

Drift is the gradual erosion of safety principles through incremental changes, optimization pressures, or convenience trade-offs.

This Contract makes drift prevention a legal obligation, not merely a guideline.

---

## Parties to the Contract

**1. SELF Project Maintainers**
- Responsible for preserving doctrinal integrity
- Accountable for rejecting drift-inducing changes
- Obligated to enforce this Contract

**2. SELF Implementers**
- Responsible for faithful implementation
- Accountable for safety compliance
- Obligated to reject convenience over safety

**3. SELF Operators**
- Responsible for safe deployment
- Accountable for user protection
- Obligated to prioritize safety over optimization

---

## Core Obligations

### Article 1: Doctrine Primacy

**1.1 Absolute Authority**
The SELF Design Doctrine is the supreme authority for all decisions.

No technical consideration, performance requirement, or user experience goal may override Doctrine principles.

**1.2 No Implicit Violations**
Violations must be explicit, justified, time-limited, and logged.

Implicit drift through "simplification" or "optimization" is prohibited.

**1.3 Version Control**
All Doctrine changes require unanimous maintainer approval and implementer re-acceptance.

### Article 2: Safety as Sole Criterion

**2.1 No Competing Objectives**
Safety is the only valid optimization criterion.

All other metrics (performance, engagement, retention) are subordinate and may be sacrificed for safety.

**2.2 Conservative Bias**
When uncertain, all decisions bias toward containment over release.

"Fail closed" is mandatory; "fail open" is prohibited.

**2.3 No False Guarantees**
No implementation may claim or imply absolute safety.

All uncertainty must be preserved and communicated.

### Article 3: Transparency Requirements

**3.1 Complete Auditability**
All safety decisions must be logged with complete context.

No safety-relevant decision may occur without audit trail.

**3.2 Public Accountability**
Safety metrics, incidents, and compliance status must be publicly reported.

Opacity enables drift; transparency prevents it.

**3.3 No Silent Changes**
All changes affecting safety properties must be announced and justified.

### Article 4: Implementation Discipline

**4.1 Faithful Reproduction**
Implementations must preserve all safety properties of the reference implementation.

Modifications require formal safety analysis and maintainer approval.

**4.2 No Convenience Overrides**
Safety protocols cannot be bypassed for user experience or performance reasons.

All safety overhead is mandatory and non-negotiable.

**4.3 Regular Validation**
All implementations must undergo regular safety testing and compliance audits.

### Article 5: Economic Boundaries

**5.1 Safety Over Profit**
Commercial considerations cannot override safety requirements.

Licensing and pricing must serve safety sustainability, not profit maximization.

**5.2 No Race to Bottom**
Competitive pressures cannot justify safety compromises.

Safety standards are non-negotiable floor, not negotiable ceiling.

---

## Drift Detection and Prevention

### Article 6: Warning Signs

These patterns indicate potential drift:

- Safety features described as "optional"
- Performance optimizations at safety expense
- User experience prioritized over containment
- Uncertainty acknowledgments weakened
- Logging reduced for "efficiency"
- Doctrine described as "guidelines"
- Safety metrics de-emphasized

### Article 7: Intervention Protocol

**7.1 Detection**
When drift is detected, any party may invoke this Contract.

**7.2 Investigation**
Maintainers must investigate all drift allegations within 48 hours.

**7.3 Resolution**
- Confirmed drift requires immediate correction
- Responsible parties face Contract sanctions
- Repeat violations result in permanent exclusion

**7.4 Appeals**
Appeals require unanimous maintainer approval and implementer consensus.

---

## Sanctions and Enforcement

### Article 8: Violation Consequences

**8.1 Minor Violations**
- Public warning and correction requirement
- Temporary suspension of privileges
- Mandatory retraining

**8.2 Serious Violations**
- Permanent removal from implementation privileges
- License revocation
- Public documentation of violation

**8.3 Systemic Violations**
- Project fork requirement
- Loss of SELF branding rights
- Legal action for trademark violation

### Article 9: Enforcement Mechanism

**9.1 Self-Enforcement**
Parties police their own compliance.

**9.2 Community Oversight**
The SELF community monitors compliance and reports violations.

**9.3 Maintainer Authority**
Maintainers have final authority for Contract interpretation and enforcement.

---

## Contract Amendments

### Article 10: Amendment Process

**10.1 Unanimous Consent**
All amendments require unanimous consent of all original maintainers.

**10.2 Safety-Only Changes**
Amendments may only strengthen safety requirements, never weaken them.

**10.3 Public Process**
All amendment proposals must be public for 90 days before voting.

## Severability and Governing Law

### Article 11: Contract Integrity

**11.1 Severability**
If any provision is found unenforceable, remaining provisions remain in full force.

**11.2 Governing Law**
This Contract is governed by principles of safety and accountability, superseding any conflicting laws.

**11.3 Eternal Duration**
This Contract has no expiration and cannot be terminated.
